# Lockedin — Legal Review Packet (Landing Page + Messaging)
**Purpose:** Provide a structured brief for external counsel to review Lockedin’s public messaging and user-facing disclosures to reduce regulatory and lawsuit risk.  
**Note:** This document is not legal advice. It is a checklist and context pack to accelerate a qualified lawyer’s review.

---

## 1) Product Summary (Plain English)
Lockedin is a commitment platform where users voluntarily lock money behind a goal. Users submit proof (notes + optional photo). If users miss commitments, penalties may apply based on a chosen tier. A portion of forfeited amounts may be redistributed to successful users as incentives, and the platform may retain a fee.

---

## 2) What We Need Counsel To Decide

### 2.1 Classification / Regulatory Posture
Please assess whether the current model could be classified as any of the following in our launch geography/geographies:
- gambling/wagering/betting product
- investment product / collective investment scheme
- money transmission / stored value / e-money / wallet custody
- consumer lending / credit / deposit-taking / bank-like product
- promotional contest / sweepstakes
- “marketplace” facilitation and platform liability exposure

### 2.2 Custody and Funds Flow
Confirm what legal implications follow from:
- users funding accounts through Paystack (NGN/kobo)
- internal ledger tracking balances and protocol locks
- platform-controlled redistribution of forfeits and fees
- withdrawals (user cash-out)

### 2.3 Required Disclosures + User Agreements
Identify what must be added/updated across:
- Terms of Service
- Privacy Policy
- “How it works” disclosure
- Risk disclosure before user stakes/locks funds
- Refund policy
- Dispute policy and timelines

### 2.4 Advertising / Marketing Claims
Review our landing page and messaging claims for:
- misleading claims (income promises, guaranteed results)
- “earn” framing (does it imply investment returns?)
- social proof claims (proof requirements)
- user testimonials compliance rules

### 2.5 KYC/AML Requirements
Confirm whether we must implement:
- KYC at onboarding vs at withdrawal
- sanctions screening
- transaction monitoring thresholds
- enhanced due diligence for certain users

---

## 3) Landing Page Messaging: Risk Areas To Review

### 3.1 Avoid “Wagering” Language
We should avoid terms like:
- bet, betting, wager, odds, winnings, gambling, jackpot

### 3.2 Avoid “Investment” Language
We should avoid terms like:
- invest, ROI, returns, yield, profit, dividends (unless lawyer-approved)

### 3.3 High-Risk Claims To Remove or Reframe
Please review and advise rewording for:
- “earn from others’ losses”
- “bank”, “institutional-grade enforcement”, “zero-trust behavioral bank”
- “guaranteed discipline”, “guaranteed results”

---

## 4) Proposed Public Positioning (Draft)
**Goal:** Position as “commitment escrow + accountability”, not gambling/investment.

Suggested phrasing:
- “You choose the rules before you commit.”
- “Your funds are locked behind your goal’s protocol.”
- “If you miss commitments, penalties apply based on your selected tier.”
- “Incentives may be distributed from forfeitures to eligible users under published rules.”
- “This is not an investment product. Results are not guaranteed.”

---

## 5) User-Facing Disclosure Blocks (Draft Requirements)

### 5.1 Pre-Stake Confirmation Modal (must be explicit)
Before locking funds, show:
- selected tier and exact penalty % and trigger conditions
- verification rules and dispute window
- what happens to forfeited amounts (fees + incentive pool)
- statement of user consent and irreversibility (as applicable)

### 5.2 Risk Disclosure (short and clear)
Minimum disclosure should cover:
- user may lose money if commitments are missed
- evidence disputes may result in penalties if rejected
- timelines and rules are binding once accepted

### 5.3 Refund Policy Disclosure
Define:
- if/when refunds are permitted
- how chargebacks are handled
- what happens if payments are “successful” but credit is delayed (reconciliation process)

## 6) Operational Policies Counsel Should Review
- dispute policy (time window, reasons, outcomes)
- anti-fraud policy (collusion, fake proof, witness abuse)
- enforcement policy (how penalties are applied, auditability)
- complaints handling and escalation workflow
