# Fundraising & Outreach Playbook (Lockedin)

This is a practical guide for fundraising: where to apply, who to reach out to, how to message them, and what to prepare.

## 1) What You’re Raising For (Clarity First)

Lockedin is building a commitment + enforcement platform where users stake per protocol, prove execution via logs, and use witnesses for accountability. The system is designed to be non-custodial and compliance-friendly.

Typical near-term funding uses:
- Hire core team (product engineering, growth, design)
- Payments reliability + trust/safety + compliance posture
- Distribution experiments (community, partnerships)
- Foundational analytics and retention loops

## 3) What You Need Ready Before Outreach

### Core Assets
- 1-page memo (what it is, why now, why you, why this market)
- Deck (10–12 slides)
- Demo (live URL + short Loom)
- Metrics snapshot (even early): activation, retention, evidence logs per user, funding conversion

### Due Diligence Preparedness
- Clear non-custodial explanation (how funds flow, what user owns)
- Payments and reconciliation overview
- Security posture basics (rate limiting, audit logs, access control)

## 4) The Narrative (How to Explain Lockedin Simply)

Investor-grade framing:
- People fail not because they lack desire; they lack enforcement and social accountability.
- Lockedin turns commitment into an enforceable protocol: stake + proof + witness authorization.
- It’s an infrastructure for “serious execution” (habits, learning, fitness, sales performance).

## 5) Who to Contact Inside Each Org

Prioritize:
- Partners or principals who invest in consumer + fintech-adjacent + emerging markets
- Operators-in-residence / platform teams (they can help with hiring and intros)

For accelerators:
- Apply through the official application
- Also get warm intros from alumni founders (highest conversion)

## 6) Outreach Message Templates

### A) Cold Email to a VC (Short)
Subject: Lockedin — enforcement layer for commitments (stake + proof + witnesses)

Hi {Name},

I’m building Lockedin: a non-custodial commitment platform where users stake per protocol, submit proof-of-execution logs, and witnesses authorize compliance.

We’re seeing {insert traction: signups / funded protocols / weekly retention / evidence logs} and are raising {pre-seed/seed} to scale product + distribution and harden payments + trust/safety.

Can I send a 1-pager + a 2-minute demo?

Thanks,
{Your name}
{Location}
{Website}

### B) Warm Intro Ask (Text/DM)
Hey {Name} — can you intro me to {Investor/Partner}? We’re building Lockedin (stake-per-protocol + proof + witnesses), and I’d love feedback / explore a pre-seed round. I’ll send a tight 1-pager.

### C) Accelerator Application Mini-Pitch (200–300 chars)
Lockedin helps people execute commitments using stake-per-protocol funding, proof logs, and witness authorization. It’s a non-custodial enforcement layer for habits and performance.

### D) Follow-up (If No Reply)
Hi {Name} — quick follow-up. Sharing the short memo + demo here: {links}. If it’s not a fit, could you point me to the right person on your team?

## 7) What to Ask For (Besides Money)

- Distribution partners (communities, orgs, influencers)
- Payments/compliance advisors
- Hiring support (lead eng, growth)
- Design partners (early power users who will pay)

## 8) Suggested Deck Outline (10–12 Slides)

1. Title (Lockedin) + one-liner
2. Problem (execution failure + lack of enforcement)
3. Solution (protocols + stake + proof + witnesses)
4. Why now (remote work, creator economy, accountability demand, fintech rails)
5. Product demo (screenshots + flow)
6. Traction (activation, funded protocols, retention, revenue if any)
7. Market (who pays; B2C + prosumer + B2B)
8. Business model (subscription + premium witnesses/coaches + teams + B2B)
9. Go-to-market (community-driven, partnerships)
10. Competition (why you win)
11. Team (why you can execute)
12. The raise (amount, runway, milestones)

## 9) Fundraising Process (Simple Operating System)

- Build a list of 80–150 targets
- Start with warm intros + alumni networks
- Run 2–3 weeks of meetings, batch scheduling
- Send weekly investor updates (traction + learnings)
- Optimize: message, demo, and metrics after every 10 calls

## 10) Notes for YC Specifically

What tends to matter most:
- Clear problem/solution
- Founder clarity and speed
- Early signs of demand (even small)
- A product that can scale globally

Suggested approach:
- Apply with a crisp demo and strong 1-liner
- Ask YC alumni for feedback on the application before submitting
